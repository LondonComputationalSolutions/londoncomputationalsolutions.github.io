Teaching and Tutoring

Steiner education

My teaching experience is firmly grounded in the teachings of Rudolf Steiner and Waldorf education. I believe that children (and adults to a certain degree) learn best when they are emotionally comfortable, when their natural interest and curiosity is awakened and tapped into, and when they are in a trusting relationship with their teacher or tutor.

I was lucky enough to have Prof Dr Peter Lutzker as my mentor while studying Steiner education. I profited even further when I helped edit his PhD “Teaching as an Art”.

My own teaching at Steiner school was always based on an artistic element. That could be a song that we would start the lesson with, or a poem we would recite. It could be a scene from a play or even an action rhyme that we would move to. Spoken word, song, drama (play) and movement have always been key essentials in Steiner education. People learn best, when they are in flow. We can not easily create flow through rationalising, analysing, dissecting a poem when we first read it. But we can create an emotional connection to it and thus a desire to 'move' with that emotion towards the poem. This act of moving could be with our voices, with our bodies or even with our hands (sculpture, drawing etc). Yet it would always allow for us to internalise the piece we are working on, to make it a part of ourselves. What has become a part of yourself, you will not lose again and it will become precious to you.

Learner types

When teaching any subject, it is essential for the teacher to quickly figure out what kind of a learner type the student might be. Does she like to stay with a single task for a long time? Or is her enthusiasm kindled by new challenges which have to change rather quickly in order for her to keep engaged? Does he need to have something physical/material he can 'grab' in order to understand a topic? Or would he rather wander through imaginary worlds and focus on their details? To know what it takes for a person to stay engaged is at least two thirds of the way to learning and memorising.
